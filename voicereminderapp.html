Voice Reminder App

A fast, vivid, and easy to use voice reminder app that allows users to create, edit, and delete voice reminders.

Tech Stack: JavaScript, CSS

User Tasks

  1. Creating/Recording a reminder.
    Description: The user records a voice message or types a reminder. The system saves the reminder, listing it in the reminders list.
  2. Deleting a reminder
    Description: The user deleted a reminder from the reminders list, and it is reflected in the reminders list.
  3. Editing a reminder
    Description: The user can edit a reminder in time, date, title, or recording.

Lo-fi Prototype

With our App prototype, we intend to collect user data in different scenarios, such as: App navigation. Creating an interface that is intuitive and useful for the user, considering other common reminder app designs. What users use voice reminders for. The reminder app is intended to help those with disabilities, students, and help others multitask. But within those users, we want to seek app competence by increasing its capabilities. This will help find more user requirements and gaps within the prototype.

User Study 1

The purpose of this experiment is to gather qualitative feedback on two low-fidelity voice reminder app prototypes. This will refine the requirements from users and their experiences with survey questions and personal experiences.

The goal is to collect feature suggestions and design feedback for the reminder-setting experience.
Hypothesis: Users will find our reminder app prototype design intuitive to use, with clarity in comparison to their preferred reminder app.

Through our experimental procedure, we will collect users' responses to the survey questions asked. This will provide us with personal and reflective data of their own experience.

    Qualitative Data:
  • Open-ended questions on their current preferred method of setting reminders.
  • Direct user feedback and verbal comments on both prototypes.
  • Notes on features requested, concussion points, or questions in the proposed prototypes.
  • Post interview questions about clarity, navigation, and overall satisfaction.
    Colection Methods:
  • Observation notes recorded by the researcher during each session.
